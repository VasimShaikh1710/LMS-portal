Bionic Hand Overview

A bionic hand is an advanced prosthetic device designed to replicate the functionality of a human hand. It uses sensors, actuators, and microcontrollers to interpret signals from the user’s body, such as muscle movements or nerve impulses.

Equipped with motors, it provides precise control for tasks like gripping, holding, and pointing. Modern bionic hands often incorporate machine learning to adapt to the user's unique movements over time. They can include features like tactile feedback, gesture recognition, and multi-grip functionality.

Built using lightweight materials and 3D printing, bionic hands are customizable for comfort and usability. Some models even integrate wireless connectivity for remote adjustments. These devices restore mobility and independence to individuals with limb loss or disabilities, making them a groundbreaking innovation in robotics and prosthetics.

Components

Servo Motor

A servo motor is a compact and precise rotary actuator used to control the angular or linear position, velocity, and acceleration. It operates using a closed-loop feedback system, ensuring accurate movements.

Arduino Board

The Arduino board is an open-source microcontroller platform designed for building electronic projects. It provides an easy-to-use hardware and software interface, making it beginner-friendly yet versatile for advanced applications.

Parts

A 3D printed bionic hand is an innovative prosthetic device designed to replicate the functions of a natural human hand. These hands often incorporate sensors and motors to provide movements such as gripping and holding objects.

USB

A Mini USB A to B cable is used to connect devices like cameras, printers, and Arduino boards to computers or other compatible devices. This cable supports data transfer and power supply, providing a reliable connection for various electronics.

Adapter

A 9V 1A adapter is a power supply device used to provide a stable 9-volt DC output with a current rating of up to 1 ampere. It is commonly used to power small electronic devices, such as Arduino boards, sensors, and motor drivers.
